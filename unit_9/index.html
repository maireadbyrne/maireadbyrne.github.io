Just Out!

It's all thanks to translator and editor Dirceu Villa who has nursed this project for 10 years whilst accomplishing even more magnificent things including a PhD in Italian and British Poetry of the 15th and 16th centuries, two new books of poetry, and the magnificent Lustra, his Ezra Pound translation. Dirceu also secured publication by Dobra Editorial, in their terrific Passagens series, and the participation of Leonardo Fróes, who wrote the thoughtful postscript, on translation as much as poetry, though the two terms are perhaps synonymous. Thank you all and especially Dirceu for your commitment and generosity.

Famosa Na Sua Cabeça

Você pode ser bem famosa na sua cabeça por alguma coisa. Laminação em ouro, digamos. Falsificação. Sou falsificadora par excellence, segundo minha própria lenda. Sou retratista-mestre de Zé Stálin. Sou uma astuta fazedora de mini-homens da caverna. Sou mais famosa na minha cabeça por cair de barcos em Bray e no Mar de Mármara & rir como se faltasse parafuso na cabeça—a mesma na qual sou famosa. Sou famosa na minha família por cozinhar & assar & mudar rápido de humor. Minha mãe era famosa por assar & por um vestido vermelho. Minha irmã era famosa por trapaças. Lá se foi essa gente famosa. Minha cabeça é uma arena pequena, mas sou a estrela lá, com as luzes acesas.

Famous In Your Head

You can be very famous in your own head for something. Gilding, for example. Forgery. I am a forger par excellence, according to my own legend. I am a master portraitist of Joe Stalin. I am a cunning maker of small cavemen. I am most famous in my head for falling out of boats in Bray and the Sea of Marmara & laughing my head off—that very same head I'm famous in. I'm also famous in my family for cooking & baking & lightning changes of mood. My mother was famous for baking & a red dress. My sister was famous for trickery. They're all gone now, those famous folk. My head is a very small arena but I'm the star there, when the lights are on.


from Famosa na sua cabeça, tradução de Dirceu Villa (São Paulo: Dobra Editorial 2015), first published in The Best of (What's Left of) Heaven)
